# L02-Q002

**Difficulty:** Medium
**Domain:** Threat Intelligence
**Objective:** 2.1 Compare vulnerability, threat, and risk concepts
**Security+ Objective Mapping:** SY0-701 Objective 2.1 - Vulnerability, threat, risk definitions

## Scenario
A security assessment reports that a missing patch is a weakness, a hacker scanning the network is a threat, and the possible theft of customer records is a concern. Which term best matches the missing patch?

## Choices
A. Risk
B. Vulnerability
C. Control
D. Incident

## Correct Answer
B

## Detailed Explanation
A missing patch is a vulnerability, which is a weakness that can be exploited by a threat.

## Why the Incorrect Options Are Wrong
- A: Risk is the potential loss, not the weakness itself.
- C: A control mitigates vulnerabilities, it is not the weakness.
- D: An incident is a realized event, not the condition of missing a patch.

## Exam Tip
Vulnerabilities are weaknesses; threats are actors or events; risk is what happens when they combine.
